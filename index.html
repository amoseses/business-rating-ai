CloudSight AI — Enterprise Computer Vision

Strong pitch · Plan: Plus
CloudSight AI is a B2B computer vision platform that reduces manufacturing defects by 94% using off-the-shelf cameras and edge inference. We have 17 enterprise contracts totaling $2.8M ARR, with 140% net revenue retention. Our product integrates with existing factory systems in under 48 hours, no custom hardware required. The global computer vision market is $19B and growing at 22% CAGR. We are a team of ex-Tesla and Intel engineers. Raising a $5M Series A to expand sales to automotive and pharmaceutical verticals.
Problem & Solution: 92/100 — Exceptionally clear problem-solution fit. The 94% defect reduction is a powerful, specific metric.
Market Opportunity: 88/100 — Well-quantified TAM with credible growth data. Mentioning total addressable market breakdown would strengthen further.
Traction: 90/100 — Strong revenue and retention metrics. 17 enterprise logos at $2.8M ARR is compelling for Series A.
Business Model: 85/100 — Clear per-seat licensing model implied. Explicit pricing and gross margin data would add confidence.
Team: 80/100 — Credible pedigree from top-tier companies. Adding specific relevant achievements would elevate further.

Recommendations

• Add your gross margin percentage — investors will want to see unit economics
• Specify how you'll use the $5M (hiring, sales, R&D breakdown)
• Include a competitive differentiation section comparing vs. legacy inspection systems

PayBridge — Cross-Border Payment Rails for SMBs

Needs work · Plan: Data
PayBridge helps small businesses send and receive international payments with lower fees than traditional banks. We use a multi-currency wallet system that settles in real-time. Our beta has 340 users and we process about $50K monthly volume. We charge 0.8% per transaction, which is cheaper than PayPal and Wise. The cross-border payments market is huge. We are based in Toronto and have a team of 5. We need funding to build out our compliance infrastructure and expand to the US market.
Problem & Solution: 70/100 — The problem is relatable but the solution description is generic. Explain why your multi-currency wallet is technically different.
Market Opportunity: 55/100 — "Market is huge" is not sufficient. Provide specific TAM, SAM, SOM figures for the SMB cross-border segment.
Traction: 60/100 — 340 beta users and $50K monthly volume is early-stage traction. Month-over-month growth rate would make this stronger.
Business Model: 68/100 — 0.8% fee is clear but need to explain customer acquisition cost, lifetime value, and how unit economics improve at scale.
Team: 50/100 — "Team of 5" without background context. Investors need to know why this team specifically can win in payments.

Recommendations

• Add specific market data: global B2B cross-border payments were $29T+ in 2025, SMB share is ~$3T
• Show month-over-month growth rate of users and volume — growth matters more than absolute numbers at early stage
• Include founding team background — prior fintech experience, exits, or relevant domain expertise
• Add your regulatory strategy — how are you handling money transmitter licenses and compliance?

GreenCart — Eco-Friendly Grocery Delivery

Needs improvement · Plan: Data
GreenCart delivers organic groceries using electric bikes in urban areas. We want to make sustainable food accessible to everyone. We launched last year and have some customers in Portland. Our app lets people order from local farmers and we deliver within 2 hours. We think people care about the environment and will pay more for eco-friendly delivery. We need money to expand to more cities and buy more bikes. Our team is passionate about sustainability.
Problem & Solution: 45/100 — Problem is broadly stated without data. How many people actually face this issue? What's wrong with existing grocery delivery options?
Market Opportunity: 35/100 — No market size, no growth data, no competitive landscape. Investors need to see the addressable market quantified.
Traction: 30/100 — "Some customers" and "launched last year" are vague. Need specific numbers: users, orders, revenue, retention, delivery times.
Business Model: 40/100 — No mention of revenue model, margins, delivery fees, or unit economics. How does each order contribute to profitability?
Team: 45/100 — "Passionate about sustainability" is not enough. What relevant experience does the team have in logistics, grocery, or startups?

Recommendations

• Add specific traction numbers: current customers, orders per week, revenue, average order value, retention rate
• Quantify the market: US organic food market is ~$70B; online grocery is growing at 15% CAGR. Use this data.
• Explain your unit economics: delivery cost per order, average margin, customer acquisition cost, path to profitability
• Include team backgrounds: prior startup, logistics, or grocery industry experience — why this team?
• Add competitive differentiation: how are you different from Instacart, Amazon Fresh, or local delivery services?
